The Dominance of Trek - A Dive into Performance and Consumer Preferences (2016-2018)

Report | Dashboard

This project examines the product and sales performance of brands/products for BikeStore from 2016-2018. The goal was to determine which products sell, what types of consumers purchase BikeStore products, and why the bicycle brand Trek dominates the space. Findings are featured in an Interactive Tableau dashboard and an accompanying business report. Recommendations and applications to drive business growth are also included at the end of the business report.

bike_store_db

Report | Queries

The goal of bike_store_db was to create an efficient relational database from scratch in PostgreSQL. The project has 3 phases: Database Creation, Data Insertion, and Querying Data. Utilizing my expertise in various query statements, I created nine tables, established relations, created user/group roles, and inserted over 1000 records. From there, I generated KPIs focused on product and employee performance to assess the success of the fictitious bike store. Intent on displaying my strength in problem-solving and creativity, I explain my thinking and techniques behind each business scenario.

NBA Multivariate Linear Regression Model

Google Colab

This was a fun project for me! I practiced my skills in multivariate linear regression while working with a data set I was naturally interested in. I'm a huge NBA fan, specifically a Golden State Warriors fan!

I asked myself: Was it possible to predict home and away points of any given team based on shooting percentages?

I built three regression models to accomplish this. One predicts home points, another predicts away points, and a final model predicts the probability of the home team winning given the current score. The highlight was my probability model, which was over 98% accurate.

US National Park Trip Planner/Recommender *In Progress

Schema

I want to create a website that directs users to reservation links for campsites in each US National Park. Users will filter down to the campsite that fits their desired trip. Options such as driving accessibility, peak months, size, remoteness, and location will allow users to find the National Park and subsequent campsite that fits them!

All the information to reserve a campsite is available, but it’s not centralized, stored consistently, easy to find, or updated in many cases. My website will solve this. Instead of surfing the web and digging through each US National Park website, users can use my “one-stop shop” to find the park that fits their trip and begin planning. This is not a solution just for the hardcore backpackers; it’s a solution for the casual camper looking for a weekend getaway, families, or anyone looking to enjoy the beauty of each National Park!

I’ve been passionate about exploring each National Park for as long as I can remember, and I want to share that with everyone!

I’ve finished all my data collection and planned my schema for a relational database. I’m currently working on web development. I have all my data, I just need to build the medium to share it with the world!
